Frequently asked questions

Common questions about Skratch Tracker.

Is Skratch Tracker a medical device?

No. Skratch Tracker is a self-tracking and signposting tool. It does not diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent disease. Always consult a healthcare professional for medical advice.

Where is my data stored?

All health data (symptoms, photos, treatments, contacts, quiz answers) is stored only on your device. Nothing is uploaded to our servers. If you choose to create an account for Signal Sharing, only your anonymous signal status is stored online — never your health details.

Do I need to create an account?

No. The app works fully without any account. An optional Sign in with Apple account is only needed if you want to use the anonymous Signal Sharing feature to alert close contacts.

What does the AI feature do?

Skratch Intelligence lets you ask questions about symptoms, treatments, and next steps in natural language. Answers are based on publicly available health guidance. It does not provide a diagnosis and is not a substitute for professional medical advice.

Is analytics data collected?

Only if you explicitly opt in. Analytics is off by default and can be changed at any time in Settings. If enabled, we collect only broad app-usage patterns (screen views, feature use, error types) — never symptom details, photos, contacts, AI conversations, or any health information.

Can I export my data for a doctor?

Yes. The GP Summary feature generates a PDF with your symptom timeline, treatments, and self-check results. You can share this with your clinician, pharmacist, or sexual health clinic.

How does Signal Sharing work?

If you create an account and link with a close contact via invite code, they receive a private notification if your concern level changes. The signal contains no health details — just a nudge that they may want to seek advice.

Can I delete all my data?

Yes. The “Clear all data” option in Settings removes everything from your device: photos, logs, quizzes, contacts, treatments, and settings. If you have an online account, you can also delete that and all associated server-side data.
